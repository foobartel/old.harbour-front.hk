Welcome Harbour Front Monthly!

Harbour Front Monthly is a new event series that will cover a variety of topics around all things mobile & responsive web design & development, discussing the latest technologies, new ideas and best practices in web design. The event format will consist of 1 or 2 presentations of approx. 30 minutes each, followed by a short Q&A and networking drinks to mingle with your industry peers afterwards.

Our second event will take place on Wednesday, SEPTEMBER 25, 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM at the Hive in Wan Chai. (HK$100 includes free drinks)

You can register via Eventbrite or at the door on the evening of the event. You can follow us on Twitter for the latest updates and we're on Meetup too.

Managing expectations by Jeffrey Broer

Jeffrey's talk isn't about coding, but about Managing Expectations in development projects. He talks about client expectations, team expectations, cultural expectations and gives tips on how to handle them.

Jeffrey Broer is the founder of Grayscale LTD (2011) a HK based Dutch Web design company. Originally schooled as a Commercial Mechanical Engineer, his knowledge and expertise cover the whole supply chain from initial product development to ultimate design marketing programs.

Talk 2: 8:10pm

Exploring Sass

Sass styling by Gregor Wilson

Sass is the most mature, stable, and powerful professional grade CSS extension language in the world.

Gregor Wilson from Pixelcabin will be giving us an introduction to Sass. He will go into detail about some more advanced tips & tricks which can be done with Sass that may not be apparent at first sight. He will will also elaborate about other frameworks and libraries that can nicely work alongside and integrate with Sass.

Originally from Scotland, Gregor spent many a long winter in his attic with the family's first computer creating websites for cars, animations and other kids at his school. While pursuing a degree in Business & Law, his love of coding never faltered, so it was only a matter of time until his passions came together at pixelcabin.
